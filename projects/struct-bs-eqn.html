Context

Numerical methods play a central role in option pricing and risk analysis. While the underlying mathematical models are well understood in theory, practical use depends on discretized implementations whose behavior is often assessed using generic numerical criteria.

This project is concerned with the relationship between numerical implementation choices and the qualitative behavior of pricing solutions. Rather than focusing on model specification or market calibration, the emphasis is on how numerical procedures themselves can influence interpretability and trust.

The work is exploratory and diagnostic in nature. It treats numerical solvers as objects of scrutiny, with the goal of better understanding their limitations and failure modes in applied financial settings

Approach

The project is structured as a sequence of controlled numerical experiments rather than a single end-to-end solution. Emphasis is placed on formulation choices, diagnostic checks, and comparative behavior across regimes, not on optimizing any one method.

Standard modeling setups are used as reference points, allowing attention to remain on numerical behavior rather than model novelty. Variants are introduced incrementally to isolate the effect of individual implementation decisions.

Throughout, the approach favors transparency and restraint. Methods are chosen to be interpretable and reproducible, with conclusions drawn conservatively and only where behavior is consistent across settings.

Implementation & Tools

The work is implemented using a small, modular computational setup designed to support controlled experimentation rather than large-scale deployment. Components are structured to allow rapid iteration on discretization choices and diagnostics while keeping the overall system easy to reason about.

Emphasis is placed on clarity and reproducibility. Numerical experiments are scripted to ensure consistent parameter control and to make comparative behavior across runs easy to inspect.

Tooling choices favor stability and transparency over performance tuning. The current implementation uses Python with standard scientific computing and visualization libraries such as numpy, pandas, and matplotlib.

Preliminary Observations

Early experiments suggest that numerical behavior can be sensitive to implementation choices in ways that are not always apparent from aggregate diagnostics or visual inspection alone. Certain effects emerge only under refinement, stress, or comparison across closely related configurations.

These observations have informed how subsequent experiments are structured, particularly with respect to isolating individual numerical decisions and examining their downstream impact. At this stage, the emphasis is on identifying patterns worth further investigation rather than drawing general conclusions.

This work is part of an ongoing manuscript and remains under active development.

Analysis Notes

Analysis focuses on controlled comparison across closely related numerical configurations to identify changes in qualitative behavior. Results are examined across parameter ranges rather than isolated runs.

Interpretation is conservative. Observations are documented as inputs to further testing, with care taken to distinguish robust behavior from discretization- or boundary-driven artifacts.

What This Demonstrates

  • Ability to treat financial PDEs as numerical systems rather than closed-form formulas
  • Careful reasoning about stability, monotonicity, and constraint preservation in discretized models
  • Experience designing diagnostics that expose numerical failure modes beyond visual inspection
  • Judgment in separating model assumptions from implementation-induced artifacts
  • Comfort working at the boundary between applied mathematics, numerical analysis, and finance
  • Clear documentation of exploratory technical work under uncertainty
